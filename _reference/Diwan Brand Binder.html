1.1 — The Name

One word.
Two meanings.
Both customers.

Diwan is a Saudi Arabic word that lives a double life. In the home it is the formal sitting room — the seat of family hospitality, the majlis. In commerce and government it is the council chamber — the seat of business and decision. The same word, in the same script, points to the two markets we serve. Native heritage. Globally understood.

Pronunciation
/diː ˈwɑːn/
Equal stress, long /aː/ on the second syllable.
Etymology
Persian dīwān → Ottoman, then Arabic. A register, an assembly, an account book — all spaces where matters of consequence are settled.
ديوان
The intimate diwan
[majlis]

A reception room. Long cushions on the floor, a guest at the right hand. The space where a Saudi family chooses to receive.

B2C — Homes
ديوان
The administrative diwan
[council]

An executive chamber. A circular table, a register, a decision. The space where leadership commits to direction.

1.3 — Voice Principles

Four rules. Read them aloud before writing anything that goes out the door.

01
Speak as a host, not a salesperson.

A diwan welcomes people in. It does not pitch them. Use language that opens a door — invitations, observations, offers — never urgency, scarcity, or hype.

Yes "Step inside. Tell us what you need."
No "Limited slots — book your transformation now!"
02
Be precise about outcomes.

Numbers, dates, and named deliverables beat adjectives. We measure ourselves in days, square meters, and signed-off rooms — so should our copy.

Yes "Move-in ready in 21 days."
No "A truly transformative experience."
03
Honor both languages equally.

Arabic and English are peers, not translations of each other. When copy is bilingual, both versions are written natively — neither is a shadow of the other.

Yes "حيث تتشكّل المساحات" set with equal weight.
No An afterthought Arabic line, smaller and grayed.
04
Let the space speak.

When a finished room is the point, get out of its way. Caption restraint. Empty margins. The interior is the headline; we are the byline.

Yes Wide image, two-line caption, no overlay.
No "Team Diwan delivers stunning excellence!"

3.3 — Bilingual Pairing

Two scripts. Equal weight. No exceptions.

Five rules
  1. Visual weight is equal. Neither language is subordinate to the other in any customer touchpoint.
  2. Arabic is set 1pt larger than its English equivalent — the x-height differs and we compensate at the font-size level, not optically.
  3. RTL Arabic blocks render right-to-left regardless of the surrounding LTR layout. No mirrored punctuation, no faux-LTR Arabic.
  4. Never use English typography for Arabic glyphs. No Latin fonts attempting Arabic shapes — that's a costume, not a brand.
  5. The brandmark DIWAN / ديوان appears together wherever space permits. They live as a pair.

4.2 — Construction Grid · Iwan Arch

Built on a 12-unit grid.

The Symbol
  • 12-unit square grid · 1 unit = x
  • Arch sits 2x from each side, 1x from top of capital line
  • Outer arc radius: 3x from center axis
  • Inner arc radius: 1.5x from center axis
  • Inner arch wall: 1x thick at base
  • Foot rests on baseline (12y / bottom edge)
The Wordmark
  • "Diwan" in Playfair Display Bold
  • Cap height = 4y (height of arch's inner opening)
  • Set distance: 1.5x from symbol's right edge
  • Optical adjustment: D sits +0.5px lower than baseline
  • Tracking: -0.01em (Playfair's defaults are slightly loose)
The negative space

The inner arch is the doorway — and the favicon. At 32×32 the symbol reduces to its silhouette and the negative inner shape is what the eye reads.

5.1 — Photography Direction

The room is the headline.

Subject matter
  • Finished interior spaces — primary subject, always
  • Real Saudi homes & commercial spaces — never staged "lifestyle"
  • Architectural details: door reveals, light through masharabiya, material grain
  • People only as inhabitants: dignified, doing nothing photogenic
Treatment
  • Natural light. Never harsh strobes.
  • Wide architectural angles. Never tight food-blogger crops.
  • Color graded toward late-afternoon warmth, not midday cool
  • Empty frame edges welcomed — never edge-to-edge content
Forbidden
  • ✕ Stock photos of "diverse business teams shaking hands"
  • ✕ AI-generated photorealistic faces (uncanny valley)
  • ✕ Heavy filters · vignettes · fake film grain
  • ✕ Imagery referencing a competitor's visual style
